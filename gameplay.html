Gameplay Systems

Echoes is built around a repeated extraction loop where preparation, combat, loot collection, extraction, and progression all feed into each other. The goal is to make every run feel dangerous, valuable, and personally transformative.

Core Loop

Prepare → Enter → Fight → Loot → Extract → Upgrade → Repeat

Each run begins in the hub, where the player prepares their loadout and chooses how much risk to take. During the run, players fight enemies, collect resources, and decide whether to keep pushing deeper or extract safely.

Key Player Decisions

  • Which weapons, gear, and consumables to bring.
  • Whether to engage enemies or avoid unnecessary risk.
  • When to extract and when to continue exploring.
  • Which Echoes to develop, purge, or preserve.

Combat System

Combat is designed around timing, stamina management, positioning, dodges, parries, and commitment. The intention is to create tension similar to Soulslike combat while supporting extraction-game risk.

Echo System

Weapons contain Echoes: traces of previous owners. By using a weapon, the player gains familiarity with it and gradually evolves through the memories and traits it carries.

Extraction Mechanics

Extraction creates the central pressure of each run. Loot and progression are only secured if the player survives and successfully escapes. Death can result in lost resources, failed progression, or Echo-related consequences.

Progression & Upgrades

Progression is tied to loot, weapon familiarity, Echo evolution, and hub upgrades. The player becomes stronger not only through equipment but through the identities and memories they absorb.

Design Intent

The gameplay is designed to make players feel cautious, ambitious, and tempted.

"Do I leave now with what I have, or risk everything for something better?"
