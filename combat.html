Combat System

Combat in Echoes combines melee, ranged combat, and tactical decision-making. The system emphasizes risk/reward, timing, stamina management, and player skill. Every combat choice also contributes to the Echo System, where repeated weapon use evolves the player’s form, stats, and abilities.

1. Melee Combat

Melee combat is designed to feel weighty, deliberate, and readable. Each weapon type has its own rhythm, range, stamina cost, and tactical role.

Weapon Types

  • Swords
  • Daggers
  • Axes
  • Spears
  • Greatswords
  • Staves

Attack Patterns

  • Light attacks: faster, lower damage, lower stamina cost.
  • Heavy attacks: slower, higher damage, higher stamina cost.
  • Combos: attack sequences that reward timing and commitment.

Reach & Stagger

  • Each weapon type has distinct reach and stagger potential.
  • Longer weapons offer safer spacing but may be slower or less flexible.
  • Heavier weapons can stagger enemies more reliably but demand stronger commitment.

2. Defense

Defensive play is built around stamina, timing, and positioning. Players can survive through blocking, parrying, dodging, or avoiding unnecessary engagements.

Blocking & Parrying

  • Blocking reduces incoming damage but drains stamina.
  • Parrying requires precise timing.
  • A successful parry staggers the enemy and opens a counterattack window.

Dodges / Rolls

  • Dodges allow players to evade attacks at a stamina cost.
  • Timing and positioning are critical.
  • Dodging too early, too late, or too often creates vulnerability.

3. Ranged Combat

Ranged combat gives players tools for spacing, pressure, and preparation, but requires aim, timing, and resource management.

  • Bows
  • Crossbows
  • Throwables
  • Trap-setting tools

Special Mechanics

  • Arrows and bolts may pierce enemies or carry elemental effects.
  • Traps allow players to manipulate the environment before or during encounters.
  • Ranged options create tactical variety without replacing the danger of close combat.

4. Stamina System

Stamina acts as the main limiter for combat actions and prevents reckless aggression.

  • Attacks consume stamina.
  • Blocking drains stamina.
  • Dodges and rolls consume stamina.
  • Heavy attacks and abilities demand larger stamina investment.

Running out of stamina reduces mobility and defensive options, forcing players to pace themselves and choose moments of aggression carefully.

5. Timing & Flow

Combat rewards reading enemy patterns, choosing the right weapon for the situation, and balancing offense with defense.

  • Skillful timing creates openings.
  • Poor stamina management creates vulnerability.
  • Reckless aggression can lead to death, corruption, or failed extraction.

6. Echo Integration

Every weapon contributes to its Echo archetype. Repeated use builds familiarity and gradually changes the player.

  • Weapon usage evolves stats, abilities, and physical traits.
  • Abilities and attack styles unlock after sufficient attunement.
  • Different weapon families push the player toward different evolutions.

Examples

  • Repeated spear use may unlock an evolved moveset focused on reach, control, and spacing.
  • Repeated dagger use may unlock stealth bonuses, faster movement, or assassination-focused traits.

7. Advanced Mechanics

  • Environmental interactions: players can use terrain, traps, and hazards to gain advantage.
  • Enemy variety: elite enemies and minibosses require adaptive strategies.
  • PvPvE integration: enemy players and AI threats can collide to create emergent combat scenarios.

Player Feel

Combat should feel weighty, deliberate, and skill-based. Successful engagement should reward timing, awareness, weapon mastery, and Echo progression.

Failure should carry meaningful consequences such as health loss, corruption, missed progression, or lost extraction opportunities.

Combat is not only a survival tool. It is one of the main ways the player becomes something else.
