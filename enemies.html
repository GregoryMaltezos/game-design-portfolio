Enemy Design

The Enemy Design section defines the threats the player encounters, how they behave, and how they interact with Echoes' combat, extraction, and risk/reward systems. Enemies are designed to teach mechanics, escalate tension, create meaningful choices, and produce memorable encounters without relying only on narrative exposition.

Common Enemies

Baseline threats encountered frequently during runs. They establish zone rhythm, teach combat fundamentals, and encourage positioning and resource management.

Elite Enemies

Stronger, high-risk enemies that appear less often. Elites create deliberate tension spikes, optional challenges, and localized risk/reward moments.

Read More →

Minibosses

Rare thematic enemies tied to specific zones. Minibosses break pacing, provide significant loot, and act as optional mini-climaxes.

Read More →

Uber Boss

One per map, designed as the run-ending challenge. The Uber Boss is highly telegraphed, extremely dangerous, and grants instant extraction and high-tier rewards.

Read More →

Player-Specific Echo Encounters

Late-game mastery encounters tied to the player's Echo progression, designed to test archetype mastery and provide prestige-based rewards.

Read More →

Corruption Variants

Any enemy can appear in a corruption-infused state, altering behavior, increasing threat, and rewarding higher-tier Echo progression. These variants create replay variety and reinforce the unstable nature of the world.

Human / Faction Enemies

Rival extractors, cultists, or scavengers that behave more like players. They increase unpredictability and simulate emergent PvPvE scenarios.

Design Purpose

Enemies support the core loop by creating pressure during exploration, forcing tactical combat decisions, threatening extraction, and rewarding players who understand positioning, timing, and risk.

Enemies are not just obstacles. They are pressure tools that shape the player’s decisions during a run.
