Enemy Design

The Enemy Design section defines the threats players encounter, how they behave, and how they interact with the combat, extraction, and progression systems. Enemies are designed to teach mechanics, escalate tension, and create memorable encounters while reinforcing the core loop and risk-versus-reward philosophy.

Baseline threats encountered frequently during runs. They establish combat rhythm, teach fundamentals, and encourage positioning and resource management.

Corruption Variants

Any enemy can appear in a corruption-infused state, altering behavior and increasing danger. Corruption variants create replay variety and reinforce the world's instability.

Human / Faction Enemies

Rival extractors, cultists, and scavengers create unpredictable encounters and reinforce the living nature of the world.

Design Purpose

Enemies are pressure tools. They support exploration, threaten extraction, encourage tactical combat, and shape the player's decisions throughout every run.

Enemies are not simply obstacles. They are the pressure that gives meaning to the player's decisions.
