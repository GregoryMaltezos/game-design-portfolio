UI / UX

The UI/UX direction for Echoes prioritizes readability, tension, and fast decision-making. Information is intentionally limited during runs so the player remains aware, uncertain, and responsible for interpreting the world through combat feedback, audio cues, environmental signals, and compact interface elements.

In-Run HUD

The in-run HUD displays only the information required for moment-to-moment decisions.

  • Health and Stamina: shown at all times, with stamina treated as critical due to its role in dodging, attacking, blocking, and combat pacing.
  • Corruption: shown as a separate meter that fills through death, instability, and certain abilities.
  • Visual Corruption Feedback: screen distortion increases as corruption rises, reinforcing danger without relying only on numbers.
  • Echo Attunement: displayed as a compact indicator tied to the currently equipped weapon.
  • Ammo, Consumables, and Cooldowns: positioned near the reticle or lower screen edge to reduce eye travel.

Combat Readability

Combat feedback is primarily communicated through animation, sound, and character reactions rather than excessive UI elements.

  • Enemy attacks use animation and audio tells.
  • Hit reactions, stamina drain, and posture changes provide feedback.
  • Bosses and elites use clear telegraphs for heavy attacks.
  • Telegraphs should be readable but not overly forgiving.
  • Damage direction indicators appear briefly when the player is hit from off-screen.

The UI avoids excessive warning icons to preserve tension and keep combat grounded.

Extraction UI

Extraction information is designed to become more visible only when it becomes relevant.

  • Extraction zones are shown on the map but not constantly highlighted on the HUD.
  • Entering an extraction area displays a clear extraction timer.
  • The timer becomes more visually aggressive as extraction progresses.
  • Sound cues and subtle screen effects reinforce urgency.
  • If enemies or players interrupt extraction, failure states are communicated clearly without pausing gameplay.

Map & Navigation

The map is functional, minimal, and focused on player planning rather than omniscient information.

  • Shows major landmarks, extraction nodes, and active or completed objectives.
  • Enemy locations are not shown in real time.
  • Audio cues and environmental signals are the primary tools for awareness.
  • Points of interest appear only after discovery or quest marking.

Menus & Meta UI

Menus are designed to be clean, fast, and readable between runs.

  • The loadout screen clearly shows weapon Echo progress, gear stats, and corruption risk.
  • Quest UI focuses on objectives and rewards rather than long narrative text.
  • NPC interaction panels use short dialogue and clear action buttons.
  • Hub menus prioritize preparation, upgrades, crafting, and Echo management.

UX Philosophy

  • Limit in-run information to preserve tension and uncertainty.
  • Make high-risk actions such as extraction, boss summoning, and corruption cleansing clearly confirmed.
  • Prioritize function over decorative fantasy styling.
  • Use visual style to support clarity rather than overwhelm the player.
  • Ensure players understand consequences before committing to irreversible or high-risk choices.
The interface should not make the player feel safe. It should make the danger readable.
