Hub & NPCs

The hub is the player’s safe location between runs. Nothing here can harm the player. It serves as the central point for upgrades, crafting, quests, planning, corruption management, and story progression. The hub is not a bustling city, but a functional outpost built to survive in a broken world.

Design Purpose

The hub acts as a preparation and reflection space. It connects the high-risk extraction loop with long-term progression, giving players a clear place to recover, upgrade, make strategic decisions, and understand how their Echo transformation is changing them.

  • Give players a safe space between dangerous runs.
  • Support upgrades, crafting, quests, and Echo management.
  • Reinforce the post-collapse setting through functional environmental design.
  • Allow NPCs to react to the player’s changing form and corruption state.
The hub is not a home. It is a place built to keep useful people alive.

Hub Layout & Functions

The hub is divided into rooms or stations, each with a clear mechanical and narrative purpose.

Forge

Weapon upgrading and crafting. Players can modify gear, apply temporary buffs, and refine Echo stabilizers. The operator is practical and focused on efficiency.

Clinic

Removes corruption and applies experimental boosters. The doctor treats the player as both a patient and a test subject, offering recovery and risky enhancement options.

Archive

Stores Echo memories and world information. The Archivist provides context for Echoes, weapon histories, and the wider world without overwhelming the player.

Requiem Gate

Unlocks at top-tier attunement. The Gatekeeper oversees Ultimate Challenges, Echo Legacy Duels, and other mastery-related encounters.

Board / Quest Area

Displays missions, contracts, and optional objectives. The manager provides logistical support, zone information, and survival guidance.

NPC Design & Behavior

NPCs serve functional roles and react to the player’s changing Echo traits. They are not heroic companions. They rely on the player for survival, resources, and access to the zones.

  • Practicality: each NPC has a clear purpose, such as forging, medical treatment, archiving, logistics, or gatekeeping.
  • Behavioral Feedback: NPCs may respond to visible Echo transformations, corruption, or hybrid traits with concern, curiosity, or discomfort.
  • Focused Dialogue: short dialogue delivers instructions, lore, or optional guidance without slowing the player down.
  • No Chosen Hero Framing: NPCs treat the player as useful, dangerous, and necessary rather than special or heroic.

Player Interaction Flow

Between runs, players navigate the hub to prepare for the next incursion and convert previous outcomes into long-term growth.

  • Upgrade weapons and armor.
  • Craft consumables, tools, and Echo stabilizers.
  • Remove corruption or apply risky experimental boosters.
  • Accept quests, track objectives, and plan zone strategies.
  • Review Echo attunement progress and legacy options at the Requiem Gate.

Design Intent

The hub should feel safe but not comfortable. It exists to support the player’s survival and progression, while reminding them that the world outside is broken and that their body is changing because of what they bring back.

After every run, the player returns changed. The hub is where those changes become choices.
