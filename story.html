Story & Lore

Echoes takes place in a world changed by an unknown event that released a strange energy into the environment. Exposure to this force destroys most minds and twists living bodies, turning entire regions into isolated forbidden zones.

The Hollowed

Some individuals react differently to the energy. Their minds remain stable, but their emotional responses disappear completely. These people are known as the Hollowed.

The Hollowed can think, speak, plan, learn, and make decisions, but they no longer feel fear, anger, joy, guilt, or pride.

Because they lack emotional reactions, their minds cannot be corrupted in the same way as ordinary people. Their bodies can still mutate or degrade, but their thoughts remain clear.

Echoes

Inside the forbidden zones, weapons absorb traces of their previous wielders. When people die holding their weapons, fragments of their instincts, habits, and emotional patterns remain behind.

These remnants are called Echoes.

  • A greatsword may carry the rage of a fallen warrior.
  • A bow may carry the patience and focus of a hunter.
  • A staff may carry the curiosity and discipline of a scholar.

An Echo is not a soul. It is a leftover pattern of behavior, memory, and emotion.

Borrowed Identity

When a Hollowed player uses a weapon, the Echo inside it begins to fill the emotional void in their mind.

With enough attunement, the player begins expressing the Echo involuntarily. A greatsword Echo may make them aggressive. A dagger Echo may make them move quietly and breathe lightly. A spear Echo may impose discipline and precision.

This is not possession. It is influence.

Transformation

Over time, emotional influence becomes physical transformation. The player’s body gradually adapts to match the characteristics of the Echo they rely on.

This is the narrative foundation of the Echo System: the player grows not through traditional leveling, but by absorbing the identities carried by the weapons they choose.

“I become what I wield.”

Extraction & Corruption

Extraction stabilizes Echo growth. Surviving a run locks in progression and helps the player maintain control over their transformation.

Dying or failing to extract destabilizes the Echo, potentially causing corruption, mutations, or temporary instability. These effects are dangerous, but they can be managed through cleansing, recovery, or purging at the hub.

Factions

Outside the zones, different factions compete for control over Echo technology and the resources hidden within the ruins.

  • Some study Echoes for research.
  • Some want to weaponize them.
  • Others believe the Hollowed represent a new form of humanity.

All of them depend on expeditions into the zones, because the most valuable materials and Echo-infused artifacts can only be retrieved from within.

Core Setting Idea

The zones are dangerous and unpredictable. Normal people cannot survive inside them. Hollowed individuals are the only ones capable of entering safely.

The player enters these zones, gathers resources, survives through extraction, grows through Echo resonance, and slowly shapes their identity around the weapons they choose to rely on.

The player begins as an empty vessel. The weapons decide what fills the void.
